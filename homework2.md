   <p>
  The ninth floor of the east building offers a sterile-like feeling, one similar to an unexpected hospital visit.
  The line of elevators leading down the hallway guides your eyes to the end of the hallway, I also like the
  yellow tile that stands out amongst the others. By cropping the photo to focus on the elevators path, the uniform pattern adds to the moment. I increased the shadows, whites, and blacks which brightened the once dulled light as well as brighten the shadowed walls. By decreasing the temperature, it added a frosty tone which brought out the sterile-like atmosphere. I experimented with both tint and vibrance, slightly decreasing the two to adjust the color to an almost Twilight-like filter. The effects panel added an almost hazed film over it. Overall, I'm happy with the end result.
  </p>
  <p>
 I was inspired by the staircase’s natural checkered pattern, as well as the parallel line of light.
 The raw image captured the contrast of the dark gray against the white, but the line of light didn’t stand out.
 As I worked in the camera raw filter, I played around with different colors, finding what I felt best fit the tone of the image.
 Increasing the contrast and highlights caused the once muted light to stand out. I debated between green and purple when testing 
 out the tint spectrum. Purple reminded me of a staircase on a night out, however, green sets an almost apocalyptic ambience. I'm content with the product as it reflects the perception of my environment.
  </p>
